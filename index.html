Your Aunt Betty always daydreamed of adventures around the world

Growing up in a small town creates a yearning to travel and Aunt Betty had it bad. From a young age, you’d more likely see her with her nose in an atlas than a picture book.

Although Aunt Betty graduated top of her class in archaeology and ancient history, she turned down job offers from universities and museums to embark on adventures around the globe, starting in North America.

She decided she wanted to visit every continent, then every country

On Aunt Betty's 24th birthday, she headed on a solo trip to conquer one of the deadliest ski ranges in the world - Whistler Mountain in British Columbia, Canada.

Halfway down the range on her first attempt, she narrowly avoided a snowslide before slipping into a gully full of wind-packed snow. Unwilling to let this setback discourage her, she made a second attempt and elegantly found her way to the bottom.

She eventually travelled to paradise

After years of trekking around North America and Europe, Aunt Betty found herself exploring tropical paradises in Central America and the Carribean.

She was able to fund her tropical adventures by helping the locals book affordable air travel and accommodation, while also teaching tourists how to scuba dive in the crystal clear waters of the West Indies.
