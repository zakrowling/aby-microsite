The Journey of Aunt Betty

Snapshots of an online travel agent and world renowned adventurer

Your Aunt Betty always dreamed of adventures around the world

Growing up in a small town creates a yearning to travel and Aunt Betty had it bad. From a young age, you’d more likely see her with her nose in an atlas than a picture book.

Although Aunt Betty graduated top of her class in archaeology and ancient history, she turned down job offers from universities and museums to embark on adventures around the globe, from Arizona to Barcelona and Peru to Kathmandu.

Times Square, New York City — December 1999

Aunt Betty celebrated the turn of the century in New York City, mesmerised by the glittery ball drop at midnight.

Flights to New York City, USA

She conquered every continent, one trip after another

On Aunt Betty's 24th birthday, she headed on a solo trip to conquer one of the deadliest ski ranges in the world - Whistler Mountain in British Columbia, Canada.

Halfway down the range on her first attempt, she narrowly avoided a snowslide before slipping into a gully full of wind-packed snow. Unwilling to let this setback discourage her, she made a second attempt and elegantly found her way to the bottom.

Cataluña, Spain — April 2006

Aunt Betty embarked on a journey through Spain, living in the historic old town of Cataluña for three months.
